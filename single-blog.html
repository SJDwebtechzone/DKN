Wastewater Treatment Technologies

Efficient and sustainable solutions for water reuse and environmental protection

Overview

Wastewater treatment technology involves processes that remove contaminants from water used in homes, industries, and agriculture, making it safe for reuse or discharge into the environment.

Sewage Water Treatment

Treats domestic wastewater to remove organic matter and pathogens for safe discharge or reuse.

Effluent Water Treatment

Treats industrial wastewater, removing chemicals, heavy metals, and toxic substances before release.

Demineralised Water

Removes dissolved minerals (salts) for industrial and laboratory use.

Deionized Water

Removes ions for high-purity applications in labs, electronics, and power plants.

Stages of Wastewater Treatment

Preliminary Treatment

Removes large solids, debris, and grit to protect downstream equipment.

Primary Treatment

Sedimentation allows solids to settle; skimming removes oils and grease from the surface.

Secondary Treatment

Biological processes like Activated Sludge, Trickling Filters, and SBR remove dissolved organics.

Tertiary Treatment

Advanced treatment using filtration, nutrient removal, disinfection, and membrane technologies.

Sustainability Considerations

Focus on energy-efficient processes, resource recovery, minimizing chemical use, reducing sludge, and smart water monitoring for sustainable wastewater management.
