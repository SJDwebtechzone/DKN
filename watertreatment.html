Stages of Wastewater Treatment

Oil Trap

The first stage where large debris, plastics, rags, and grit are removed to prevent equipment damage.

Primary Treatment

Sedimentation to settle suspended solids and skimming to remove oils and grease.

Secondary Treatment

Secondary treatment uses biological processes to break down dissolved and suspended organic matter.

Tertiary Treatment

This final polishing stage removes remaining nutrients, pathogens, and fine particles.

Emerging and Advanced Waste Water Treatment Technology

Emerging and advanced wastewater treatment technologies represent next-generation process solutions engineered to overcome the operational and performance limitations of conventional treatment systems. These technologies deliver higher treatment efficiency, advanced contaminant removal, and improved process stability, enabling consistent achievement of stringent discharge and reuse standards under varying influent conditions.


Advanced treatment systems are capable of removing complex pollutants, including nutrients, refractory organic compounds, and micropollutants, while improving system reliability and operational resilience. Through the integration of resource recovery, energy optimization, and water reuse processes, these technologies support sustainable wastewater management and play a critical role in meeting the demands of large-scale municipal and industrial applications, ensuring long-term environmental protection and regulatory compliance.
